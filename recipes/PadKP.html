Pad Kra Pao Recipe

What is Pad Kra Pao?

Pad kra pao is a stir fry of meat(typically ground meat), holy basil, and lots of chilies. It's served on rice, and usually with a fried egg on top.

Ingredients

  • Garlic
  • Thai chilis
  • Mild red chilies
  • Ground beef, regular or lean.
  • Fish sauce
  • Sugar
  • Unsalted beef stock, chicken or water
  • Ground black pepper
  • Holy basil
  • Eggs
  • Oil for stir frying
  • Jasmine rice
  • Prik nam pla

How to make Pad Kra Pao the traditional way

  1. In a mortar and pestle, pound the Thai chilies into a fine paste. Add the garlic and the chopped mild chilies and pound just into a rough pastes.
  2. Heat a wok over high heat, then add the beef and spread it out. Sear the beef without moving it until the underside is browned. Toss and keep cooking until liquid from the beef starts to come out.
  3. Let the beef cook, stirring occasionally, until all liquid has evaporated and the beef starts to sizzle and brown further to develop flavour.
  4. Push the beef to one side of the pan, add a little more oil and add the garlic and chili mixture, then saute until garlic starts to turn golden.
  5. Toss the garlic with the beef then add about half of the stock or water, fish sauce and sugar: toss to mix well.
  6. Add the julienned mild chiliesnd toss for 30 econds: if it's looking to dry, add a splash more water or stock.
  7. Turn off the heat, add the basil and mix just until wilted. Taste and adjust seasoning with more sugar or fish sauce as needed.
  8. To fry eggs with crispy whites and runny yolk heat about 1/4 inch of frying oil to a small nonstick frying pan over a medium-high heat. Once hot, add only the egg white and let it fry until the edges start to turn brown, then using a spoon, push the middle of the white down to make an indent for the yolk to sit in. Break any bubbles that form in the center. If you want a firmer yolk, simply fry the whole egg.
  9. Once the egg white is browned around the edges, carefully place the yolk into the indentation on top of the white.
  10. Use a spoon to baste some oil around the edges of the egg yolk to help it stick. Let it cook for a bit longer if you prefer the yolk to be a bit more set.
  11. Use a wire skimmer to remove from the pan and allow to drain.
  12. Spoon pad kra pao over some jasmine rice, and top with a fried egg. You can then drizzle the prik nam pla on top of the egg.

How to make Prik Nam Pla

  1. Combine chopped chilies and fish sauce in a small bowl. You can also add thinly sliced garlic and/or shallots if you want to make a fancier one. You can add as many chilies as you like.
  2. Add a squeeze of lime for some acidity.
